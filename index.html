Critical Findings

6 out of 9 facilities showed immediate net losses after HRH cuts (Sep-25 to Oct-25).

Major referral centers (Moi Teaching Referral and JOTRH) experienced the most significant declines.

Coptic Hospital reversed from +102 gains to -33 losses immediately post-cuts.

Resilience Examples

Port Reitz Hospital consistently gained patients (+7 total) despite challenges.

Bomu Medical recovered from initial losses, adding 61 patients after Sep-25.

3 facilities showed ability to grow even after HRH cuts.

Overall Impact

Cumulative net loss: -287 patients across all facilities from baseline.

5/9 facilities ended with net losses compared to Dec-24 baseline.

Data suggests referral hospitals are most vulnerable to HRH cuts.

Key Analysis Summary

The data reveals that while some facilities showed resilience initially, system-wide HRH and budget cuts in FY26Q1 have begun to undermine TX_Curr stability across most facilities. The immediate post-cut period (Sep-25 to Oct-25) shows concerning reversals in facilities that were previously gaining patients.

Most Vulnerable: MTRH (-146 total), JOTRH (-37), Coptic (+69 to +36)
Most Resilient: Port Reitz (+7), Bomu (+61 after Sep)
Data Lag Concern: Oct-25 may not yet reflect full impact
